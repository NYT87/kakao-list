## Design Context

### Users
Regular people checking and managing their saved lists from Kakao Map. They are not power users or admin operators. The interface should help them quickly scan saved places, open the right list, and review notes without cognitive overhead.

### Brand Personality
Simple, accessible, clean.

The interface should feel calm, direct, and understandable on first contact. It should avoid density for its own sake and should never feel complex, overloaded, or overly technical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Primary visual reference: Kakao Map.

The product should feel familiar to Kakao Map users, especially in clarity and approachability, while still being its own lightweight companion surface. Both light and dark themes should be supported, with light mode treated as a first-class experience at minimum.

Anti-direction:
- complex
- overly decorative
- visually noisy
- dense “pro tool” dashboards

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize immediate comprehension over visual novelty.
2. Keep list browsing fast, obvious, and low-friction for non-technical users.
3. Use clean hierarchy and restrained styling so saved places and notes remain the focus.
4. Treat accessibility as a default, not an enhancement.
5. Keep the interface visually aligned with the familiarity of Kakao Map rather than pushing into abstract or experimental UI patterns.
